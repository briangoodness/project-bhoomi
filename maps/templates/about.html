Project Background
Project Bhoomi is intended as a contribution to the world of International Development. Traditional sources of information, such as census data, are often out of date in the developing world and thus are not representative of current conditions. This paucity of information has afforded an opportunity for researchers to explore the possibility of supplementing traditional data sources with new forms of digital data.


This project aims to build upon work done by a team of researchers led by Neal Jean, a PhD student at Stanford. Jean has demonstrated the power of applying machine learning algorithms on satellite imagery in order to determine economic conditions in Sub Saharan Africa. We have worked to reproduce Jean's work, and to create an interactive information portal that could be used by stakeholders and decision makers in the International Development and International Aid arenas.

The Data

Daylight Satellite Imagery

The majority of data used in this project is daylight satellite imagery. We currently have nearly 3 million satellite images, comprising the entirety of the countries Rwanda, Ghana, Tanzania, and Malawi.

Nightlight Satellite Imagery

We also use 'nightlights' data, which is satellite imagery of the planet at night.

Survey Data

In addition, we are using Demographic and Health Survey (DHS) data. This data provides us with 'ground truth' information that allows us to evaluate the performance of our machine learning and regression models.

Methodology

Machine Learning

Accepting nighlight satellite imagery as a proxy for wealth, we take a pre-trained convolutional neural net and use it to extract features of daylight satellite imagery that are predictive of an area's wealth. Once the features are extracted, we are able to use them to run a regression in order to predict the wealth of different regions.

User Research

A thorough research process which included both secondary and primary research, to better understand the development space. The research process continued with contextual inquiries, interviews, and usability testing of our demo as the project progressed.
